Welcome to
Honolulu

Honolulu, the capital and largest city of Hawaii, is located on the southeastern coast of the island of Oahu. As of the most recent estimates, the city's population is approximately 350,000 residents. It is classified as the urban part of Oahu. Known for its beautiful beaches, vibrant culture, and historic landmarks, Honolulu is a major hub for tourism, business, and military defense.

History of Honululu

Honolulu became a major port and trade center by the early 19th century and was officially incorporated as a city in 1907. The city became the capital of the Kingdom of Hawaii in 1845, and it remains the administrative center of the state of Hawaii today.

Economy and Income

Honolulu's economy is diverse, driven by tourism, military defense, and international business. The city serves as a gateway to the Pacific and a significant commercial and financial hub. The median household income in Honolulu is approximately $85,000 per year, reflecting the city's relatively high cost of living compared to the mainland United States.

Notable Attractions:

Honolulu offers a variety of attractions for visitors and residents:

  1. Waikiki Beach: Renowned for its surfing, shopping, dining, and nightlife.
  2. Diamond Head: A volcanic crater offering hiking trails and panoramic views of the city and ocean.
  3. Pearl Harbor: Historic site with memorials and museums, including the USS Arizona Memorial.
  4. Iolani Palace: The only royal palace in the United States, former residence of Hawaiian monarchs.
  5. Ala Moana Center: One of the largest open-air shopping centers in the world.
